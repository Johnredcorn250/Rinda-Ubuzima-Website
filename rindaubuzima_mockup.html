Who we are

Rwanda's trusted
health research partner

From vaccine trials to community outreach, we bridge the gap between scientific evidence and real-world health impact — built on 25 years of work in Rwanda and East Africa.

Founded in 2001 by Prof. Joep Lange of the University of Amsterdam and registered as a Local NGO by the Rwanda Governance Board, Rinda Ubuzima has grown from a single project into a nationally recognised research institution.

We work in close collaboration with the Rwanda Ministry of Health, national institutions, and global partners — conducting ethical, rigorous research that directly informs health policy and community practice.

What we do

Areas of expertise

Comprehensive research capabilities spanning the full spectrum from laboratory science to community-level health promotion.

💉
Clinical Research
Vaccine trials, drug trials, and point-of-care diagnostics for malaria, HIV/AIDS, COVID-19, and reproductive health.
🤝
Community Engagement
Building trust and promoting health through meaningful, sustainable community partnerships across all districts.
📊
Data Management
Robust data collection, management, and analysis systems supporting multi-site clinical research.
🔭
Social Science Research
Understanding health behaviours and systems through rigorous qualitative and quantitative social science methods.
🧪
Laboratory Services
State-of-the-art laboratory facilities with ISO-aligned quality control procedures and trained laboratory staff.
🎓
Capacity Building
Strengthening local research capabilities through training, mentorship, and institutional partnerships.
