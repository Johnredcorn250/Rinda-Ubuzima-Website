Who We Are

Built on a Clear Mission

Rinda Ubuzima was established in 2001 and registered as a Local Non-Governmental Organization by the Rwanda Governance Board. Our mission is simple but far-reaching: to execute all activities that promote health in Rwanda — through clinical trials, social science studies, and community engagement. We serve as a trusted implementing partner for the Rwanda Ministry of Health and a wide range of international research institutions.

Clinical Research

Vaccine & Drug Trials

We have completed 14 clinical trials targeting Malaria, HIV/AIDS, COVID-19, Ebola, and Reproductive Health. Our four research sites operate to international ethical and quality standards, making Rwanda an attractive and credible destination for global health research.

Our Journey

Key Milestones

A selection of the most significant moments in Rinda Ubuzima's 25-year history.

2001
Foundation of Rinda Ubuzima
Rinda Ubuzima is established and registered as a Local NGO by the Rwanda Governance Board, with a mandate to promote health in Rwanda through research and community engagement.
2014
Female & Family [e] Education Program Launched
In partnership with Health[e] Foundation and funded by Merck for Mothers, Rinda Ubuzima begins training nurses, midwives, and Community Health Workers to strengthen maternal and newborn care across Rwanda.
2016
Program Receives National Accreditation
The Female & Family [e] Education program is accredited by the Rwandan Midwives Association.
2019
UMURINZI Ebola Vaccination Program
The Government of Rwanda entrusts Rinda Ubuzima to lead community engagement for the UMURINZI Program, a voluntary Ebola vaccination campaign launched in Rubavu and Rusizi districts.
2020–2021
e-ASCov COVID-19 Digital Response Project
Commissioned by the Rwanda Biomedical Centre, Rinda Ubuzima trains 400 Community Health Workers across 4 districts with digital tools to manage the COVID-19 response.
2026
25 Years of Service & Ongoing Trials
With 14 completed clinical trials and multiple ongoing studies, Rinda Ubuzima celebrates 25 years of advancing health research and community well-being in Rwanda and the East & Central Africa region.

Community Health

Community Engagement & Outreach

Community engagement is not an afterthought at Rinda Ubuzima — it is central to everything we do. We believe that sustainable health outcomes are only possible when communities understand, trust, and participate in the research and programs designed to serve them.

1

Mobilization & Sensitization

Our teams conduct grassroots mobilization using community leaders, health workers, and trusted local voices to raise awareness and reduce hesitancy around health programs and clinical trials.

2

Recruitment & Informed Consent

We follow rigorous ethical protocols for participant recruitment. Every participant receives comprehensive information in their language before providing voluntary informed consent.

3

Retention & Follow-Up

Long-term retention is critical to research quality. Our community teams maintain ongoing relationships with participants through home visits, SMS follow-ups, and community liaison networks.

4

Multi-Channel Communication

We reach communities through radio, community meetings, digital tools, CHW networks, and trusted local organizations.
