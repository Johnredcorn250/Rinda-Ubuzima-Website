Who We Are

Built on a Clear Mission

Rinda Ubuzima was established in 2001 and registered as a Local Non-Governmental Organization by the Rwanda Governance Board. Our mission is simple but far-reaching: to execute all activities that promote health in Rwanda — through clinical trials, social science studies, and community engagement. We serve as a trusted implementing partner for the Rwanda Ministry of Health and a wide range of international research institutions.

Why Rwanda

Rwanda as a Research Destination

Rwanda's leadership in clinical research is well-established. Rinda Ubuzima operates at the heart of this ecosystem.

Regulatory Environment

Fast-Track Administrative Approvals

When all requirements are met, decision-makers in Rwanda grant research authorizations within a significantly shorter timeframe compared to other countries in the sub-region. This makes Rwanda an efficient and attractive location for time-sensitive clinical research.

Community Health

Community Engagement & Outreach

Community engagement is not an afterthought at Rinda Ubuzima — it is central to everything we do. We believe that sustainable health outcomes are only possible when communities understand, trust, and participate in the research and programs designed to serve them.

1

Mobilization & Sensitization

Our teams conduct grassroots mobilization using community leaders, health workers, and trusted local voices to raise awareness and reduce hesitancy around health programs and clinical trials.

2

Recruitment & Informed Consent

We follow rigorous ethical protocols for participant recruitment. Every participant receives comprehensive information in their language before providing voluntary informed consent.

3

Retention & Follow-Up

Long-term retention is critical to research quality. Our community teams maintain ongoing relationships with participants through home visits, SMS follow-ups, and community liaison networks.

4

Multi-Channel Communication

We reach communities through radio, community meetings, digital tools, CHW networks, and trusted local organizations — adapting our channels to each district's specific context.

Key Project

The UMURINZI Ebola Vaccination Program

One of Rinda Ubuzima's most significant community engagement mandates — trusted by the Rwanda Ministry of Health to reach thousands of people in the country's highest-risk border zones.

Launched December 2019

Voluntary Ebola Vaccination Campaign — Rubavu & Rusizi Districts

Concerned about the spread of Ebola from the neighbouring Democratic Republic of Congo, the Government of Rwanda launched the UMURINZI Program. The Ministry of Health entrusted Rinda Ubuzima to lead all community engagement activities — mobilization, sensitization, recruitment, and retention — across 14 vaccination sites in Rubavu and Rusizi Districts.

Using the Johnson & Johnson two-dose Ebola vaccine (Ad26.ZEBOV and MVA-BN®-Filo), approved by Rwanda FDA, the program ran for nearly two years. Rinda Ubuzima adapted its outreach strategies mid-campaign to accommodate COVID-19 restrictions — demonstrating organisational resilience and community trust.

Key Project

e-ASCov: Digital COVID-19 Response

Commissioned by the Rwanda Biomedical Centre, this project demonstrated how digital tools and trained Community Health Workers can transform outbreak management.

September 2020 – January 2021

Equipping 400 CHWs with Digital Outbreak Management Tools

The e-ASCov Project was designed to assess the involvement of Community Health Workers (CHWs) in managing the COVID-19 pandemic in Rwanda using digital tools. Rinda Ubuzima selected and trained 400 CHWs from four districts — Gasabo, Nyarugenge, Kirehe, and Rusizi — chosen based on high COVID-19 case loads at the time.

Training covered: COVID-19 transmission and symptoms, mental health support for patients and families, correct use of PPEs, effective health communication, and use of the e-ASCov digital application. Through this project, CHWs collected community health information and reached over 6,000 people, identifying individuals with critical conditions for timely referral.
