Who We Are

A Trusted Partner in Health Research Since 2001

Rinda Ubuzima is a Rwanda-based Local Non-Governmental Organization registered with the Rwanda Governance Board. With a strong countrywide footprint and regional engagement in East and Central Africa, we translate scientific evidence into practical health solutions.


Our mission is to execute all activities that promote health in Rwanda — through clinical trials, social science studies, and community engagement. We bring together researchers globally and work closely with the Rwanda Ministry of Health to tackle critical public health challenges.

Our Work

Key Activities & Projects

From landmark clinical trials to national community health campaigns, here is a selection of the real work Rinda Ubuzima has delivered.

Community Engagement · 2019–2021

UMURINZI Ebola Vaccination Program

Entrusted by the Rwanda Ministry of Health, Rinda Ubuzima led all community mobilisation, sensitisation, recruitment, and retention activities for Rwanda's voluntary Ebola vaccination campaign. We operated 14 vaccination sites across Rubavu and Rusizi Districts, reaching high-risk border communities with the Johnson & Johnson two-dose Ebola vaccine.

Clinical Research · HIV Prevention

Dapivirine Ring Study — Women-Centred HIV Prevention

Rinda Ubuzima has been a key clinical trial site for the Dapivirine Vaginal Ring — a women-initiated HIV prevention tool. Following a positive scientific opinion from the European Medicines Agency (EMA) in 2020, the Dapivirine Ring was approved by Rwanda FDA, giving Rwandan women a new choice in HIV prevention. Our Kigali clinic and laboratory led participant recruitment, counselling, and follow-up throughout the study.

Learn More →
Health Digitalization · 2020–2021

e-ASCov — Digital COVID-19 Response

Commissioned by the Rwanda Biomedical Centre, Rinda Ubuzima trained 400 Community Health Workers across Gasabo, Nyarugenge, Kirehe, and Rusizi districts with digital tools to manage Rwanda's COVID-19 pandemic response. CHWs used the e-ASCov application to conduct community surveillance, reaching over 6,000 people and identifying critical cases for timely referral.

Learn More →
Clinical Research · Reproductive Health

RingPlus & KARISMA Studies — Reproductive Health

Rinda Ubuzima conducted the RingPlus Study (2013–2014), a randomised clinical trial evaluating the safety and acceptability of a contraceptive vaginal ring among Rwandan women, in partnership with the Institute of Tropical Medicine (Antwerp) and the University of Liverpool. The KARISMA Study is among our ongoing research programmes further advancing women's reproductive health choices in Rwanda.

Dapivirine Ring Approved in Rwanda for HIV Prevention

Following a positive scientific opinion from the European Medicines Agency (EMA) in 2020, the Dapivirine Vaginal Ring — a women-initiated HIV prevention tool studied at our Kigali site — received Rwanda FDA approval, expanding prevention choices for Rwandan women.

Recognition
December 2022

Rinda Ubuzima Recognised at CPHIA 2022 in Kigali

At the Conference on Public Health in Africa (CPHIA 2022), Rinda Ubuzima was awarded 2nd Runner-Up Exhibitor. Our booth highlighted ongoing clinical trials on COVID-19 vaccines and malaria, and drew significant attention to the Dapivirine Ring HIV prevention study. CEPI representatives also visited our Kigali research site during the conference.
