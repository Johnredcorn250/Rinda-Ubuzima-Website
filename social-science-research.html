Overview

Social Science Research

Rinda Ubuzima’s work sits at the intersection of research and real-world implementation. Social and behavioural insight matters because communities, systems, trust, and uptake shape whether health interventions succeed beyond protocol design alone.

What this includes

  • Behavioural and community insight
  • Understanding barriers to participation and uptake
  • Context-aware implementation support
  • Community-centred research design considerations
  • Linking technical programmes to lived realities
Why It Matters

Capability that supports trusted delivery

🔍

Better design

Research and programmes are stronger when they respond to how people actually experience care, trust institutions, and navigate health systems.

🔍

Improved uptake

Social insight helps explain why participation, adherence, and follow-up succeed in some contexts and fail in others.

🔍

Implementation relevance

For public health delivery, behavioural understanding is often what turns a technically sound design into an effective programme.
